Wanted to share an update on the zebrafish developmental splicing project. We now have a working end-to-end pipeline and initial results from the White et al. (2017) dataset (ENA project PRJEB7244, 276 samples across 6 developmental timepoints).
I attached the key output files for reference (timepoint trend plot, poison exon heatmap, correlation plot, NMD feature plot, and candidate table).


Pipeline Overview

We built a 3-stage pipeline that runs on Quest via Slurm with a local web UI for job submission and result visualization:

  1. QC & Trimming — fastp adapter trimming + FastQC/MultiQC quality reports
  2. STAR Alignment — alignment to GRCz12 (NCBI Danio rerio genome assembly)
  3. LeafCutter2 Analysis — splice junction clustering, classification (productive vs. unproductive), and downstream poison exon analysis

All 276 samples (46 per timepoint: 24hpf, 30hpf, 48hpf, 72hpf, 4dpf, 5dpf) have been processed through all three stages.


Key Results

Global Unproductive Splicing Trend

The mean percentage of unproductive (UP) splice junctions decreases across development:

  24 hpf  —  0.33%
  30 hpf  —  0.33%
  48 hpf  —  0.28%
  72 hpf  —  0.31%
  4 dpf   —  0.26%
  5 dpf   —  0.27%

Spearman rho = -0.83, p = 0.042 — a statistically significant negative correlation between developmental time and unproductive splicing. This is consistent with the idea that splicing regulation tightens as embryonic cell types commit and differentiate.

Poison Exon Candidate Identification

We implemented a poison exon discovery module that identifies UP junctions whose inclusion (PSI) significantly correlates with developmental time, then filters by NMD eligibility (PTC >50nt from last exon-exon junction):

  - 86 UP junctions with measurable PSI across timepoints
  - 21 statistically significant (Spearman p < 0.05)
  - 13 NMD-eligible (PTC distance criterion met)
  - 2 candidates passing both filters: cald1a (caldesmon) and thrap3a (thyroid hormone receptor-associated protein 3)

Notable high-confidence hits (significant but lacking full NMD annotation in the current GTF):

  - krt91 — PSI drops from 85% to 18% across development (rho = -1.0)
  - trdn — PSI drops from 17% to 0.8% (rho = -1.0)
  - hnrnpc and hnrnpa1b — known splicing regulators with developmentally regulated UP junctions (rho = -0.89)


Bug Fixes During Development

Two significant issues were identified and fixed in LeafCutter2 during this work:

1. GTF biotype mismatch — The NCBI GRCz12 GTF uses "mRNA" as the transcript biotype rather than Ensembl's "protein_coding". LeafCutter2 expects the Ensembl convention. We implemented automatic GTF normalization that detects and converts NCBI-style annotations for both GRCz11 and GRCz12.

2. Coordinate system mismatch — LeafCutter2's junction classifier had an off-by-one error when matching clustered junctions to GTF annotations, causing the majority of junctions to be misclassified as unproductive. After fixing the coordinate conversion, the unproductive rate dropped to the biologically plausible ~0.3% range.
